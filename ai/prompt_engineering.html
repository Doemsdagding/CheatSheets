Chain-of-Though (CoT)
CoT prompting is a simple instruction that explicitly tells the model to reason out its solution step-by-step before providing the final answer, significantly improving accuracy on complex reasoning tasks.

How
CoT prompting is the easiest way to tap into the model's analytical capabilities. By inserting the command, "Let's think step-by-step," or a similar phrase, you compel the AI to process the complex request sequentially, dedicating separate mental steps to sub-problems rather than trying to jump directly to the answer. This slows down the generation process just enough to access the model's stronger reasoning abilities, making it less likely to make logical or calculation errors.

Example
"I have a 1,000 budget for Python books. Book A costs 20 and Book B costs 45. If I buy 10 copies of Book A, how many copies of Book B can I buy with the remaining money? Think step-by-step before providing the final number.

Few-Shot Prompting and One-shot prompting
Few-Shot and One-Shot prompting involves providing the model with one or more fully completed examples of input and the desired output to guarantee the model adheres to a specific format or style.(this builds on the example attachements card see earlier)

How
This technique works by giving the AI demonstrations of the task it needs to perform. Rather than relying solely on abstract instructions, you provide a model to follow. One-Shot means providing one example pair, while Few-Shot means providing several. This is particularly effective when you need the output to be in a non-standard or highly consistent format‚Äîlike a very specific table structure, JSON schema, or tone of voice‚Äîthat the AI might not generate reliably from text instructions alone.

ReAct prompting
ReAct prompts force the model to alternate between internal reasoning (Thought) and external output (Action), allowing it to plan steps, justify decisions, and minimize errors in complex, multi-step tasks.

How
ReAct is a structural framework where you explicitly instruct the AI to follow a cycle of thinking before producing its final output. This mimics human problem-solving: the AI states its Thought (e.g., "I need to check the data types first to see what kind of cleaning is needed"), then takes an Action (e.g., writing the code to check types), observes the result, and iterates. This internal accountability drastically improves the accuracy and reliability of the final result by forcing the model to validate its own step

XML tags
Using XML tags in your prompts is a form of advanced prompt engineering that provides structure, clarity, and control to the Large Language Model (LLM). It works by explicitly telling the AI where the instructions end and the data, context, or examples begin.

How
XML-style tags (like <instructions>, <context>, and <data>) function as explicit delimiters, which are especially powerful in complex prompts:
  •     Enhanced Clarity and Boundary Setting: Tags create clear boundaries that prevent different parts of the prompt from "contaminating" each other. For example, if your raw input data accidentally looks like an instruction, the tags (<data>...data...</data>) tell the AI to treat it as content to be processed, not a command to be executed.
  •     Improved Consistency and Control: By segmenting the prompt into labeled blocks, you encourage the AI to process each part systematically. This leads to more consistent, comprehensive, and logically structured responses.
  •     Facilitates Advanced Techniques: Tags are essential for techniques like Chain-of-Thought (CoT) and Few-Shot Prompting, allowing you to logically group examples (<example_1>) or separate the reasoning process (<thinking>) from the final answer (<output>).
  •     Easier Post-Processing: You can instruct the AI to also use XML tags in its output (e.g., "Place your final summary inside <summary> tags."). This makes it much easier for you or a downstream script to extract specific pieces of information from the AI's response.

Tag (examples)

Purpose
<instructions> The primary directives, rules, or tasks the AI must perform.
<context> Background information, previous conversation turns, or facts the AI needs to understand the situation.
<data> Contains the raw input text, document, or data the AI needs to process or analyze.
<example>/<examples> Encloses the Few-Shot or One-Shot input/output pairs to demonstrate the required format.
<output_format> Specifies the exact structure, style, or markdown required for the AI's final response.

Example
<role> You are a financial analyst specializing in risk assessment. </role>

<instructions>
Analyze the provided client notes. Identify the two most significant risks and the one key opportunity. Present your findings in the <answer> tags.
</instructions>

<data>
Client notes: Q4 spending was $2.1M. Project A is delayed by 3 weeks due to staff turnover. However, the new patent application for the European market was just approved.
</data>

<answer>
[AI's final response will go here, structured as requested.]
</answer>
